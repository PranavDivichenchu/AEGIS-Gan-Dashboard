    About AegisGAN

    AegisGAN is a senior research thesis developed at the Academy of Engineering and Technology by Pranav Divichenchu. The project applies generative adversarial networks trained on MEROPS cleavage data to computationally design novel peptide inhibitors targeting the sepsis protease cascade.

    Three GAN architectures were implemented and benchmarked: WGAN-GP, cGAN, and SupremeGAN — a novel architecture combining Spectral Normalization with 4-head Self-Attention. Generated sequences were validated through ESMFold 3D structure prediction and AutoDock Vina rigid molecular docking.

    Validated against 11,551 MEROPS cleavage events
    TOST bioequivalence bounds met across all 4 enzyme classes
    $949 wet-lab synthesis plan costed for Kallikrein 2 inhibitor
    Presented at AET Senior Research & RSEF 2026
