Parkinson’s Disease Digital Twin for Progression: 

Essentially, I want to create a “twin” of a specific patient with Parkinson’s Disease. It will stimulate how the disease will progress over time. The Input data will range from transcriptomics, proteomics, and clinical features. The twin will track and predict the trajection of the disease trajectory - how quickly will symptoms worsen, which treatments might help. This is essential because Parkinson’s identification is fairly easy, however what stage the disease is in is very difficult. Most research with Parkinson’s relies on diagnosis or static risk scores, but this model will have dynamic projection.  This could also help uncover new biomarkers that are related to fast progression. This also enables the exploration of potential interventions and treatment outcomes in silico. 


Adaptive Early Warning System for Sepsis:

I want to create a real-time early warning system for sepsis by integrating blood-based transcriptomic and proteomic data with clinical vital signs as well as laboratory results. Sepsis is one of the most difficult diseases to detect early, because its symptoms overlap with non-infectious inflammatory conditions. Furthermore, the patient data is often incomplete or incorrectly sampled. This invention is important because it uses a fusion of multi-omics and temporal patient data into a graph-based AI model that is able to handle missing information. It can alert clinicians to sepsis before it fully develops and becomes untreatable. This system has the potential to reduce sepsis mortality rates by allowing for intervention before it is too late. 

Synthetic Biomarker Discovery: 

Using Generative AI models, I want to leverage the ability to create or make up synthetic biomarkers for diseases like sepsis, neurodegeneration, or autoimmune disorders. Instead of focusing only on known biomarkers, the model generates new, hypothetical biomarkers that maximize the disease's detectability and could also enhance the accuracy of diagnosis. The novelty of the approach is essentially the AI-driven biomarker invention itself, providing a new strategy to discover signals that have not been observed and commonly have been overlooked in the world. This methodology would revolutionize early detection, and open new avenues for understanding disease mechanisms.
